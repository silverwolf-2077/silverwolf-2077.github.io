Cohort Analysis of Mental Health, Smoking and Type 2 Diabetes Risk: A Cox Survival Study

Type II Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes (T2D) is a major global public health problem and accounts for approximately 90%–95% of all diabetes cases among the more than 38 million affected individuals in the United States. As a chronic condition, T2D is associated with substantial morbidity and an average reduction in life expectancy of around ten years. (CDC, 2024)

National Prevalence

Upon data analysis, we condensed the cholesterol risk across different locations in the United States from the PLACES: Local Data for Better Health dataset (sources, part 1), identifying key behavioral factors predominantly associated with the risk through correlation analysis.

Cholesterol-Associated Factors

After utilizing NHANCES dataset (sources, part 2), we made a in-depth Exploratory Analysis, and at last we pinpointed the primary influencers of cholesterol and constructed the final Build Model for predicting individual cholesterol levels.
